# Ai. Again. Again. And Again...

I will be honest here. I am tired. Everytime I open my inbox, watch my favrotie podcasts, see a new news article headline, or find someone venting on Reddit or Bluesky, there always seems to be something in common around me. AI. AI. AI. Never before did I imagine that a 2-letter acronym would become the bane of my existence and a nearly consistent pain point. AI has permeated all throughout my life, it seems. It’s all over the place… and there never seems to be any respite from it, unless I were to turn off my phone and take a walk away from my machines.

I don’t hate AI, contrary to how I sound when I write those charged posts on Bluesky or vent verbally to my co-workers on the latest memo on the subject. What I have actually grown to hate is the current narrative around it. Right now, there seems to be a common thread, a thought around the technology from the people who are leaders in the machine learning space and managers of companies within the technosphere. The narrative reads like this: “AI is GOD and AI will solve all the problems, it will indefinately improve and raise productivity! Number go up!”

I still have yet to see it really be all that.

Sure, the AI tooling does produce _some_ results. It can sure spit out a lot of text, and some of it might just work (in the case of the latest and greatest coding models). However, I don’t think we are really thinking about the _should_ when it comes to the technology being injected into so many people’s livelihoods. Right now, it feels like there’s a whole lot of shit throwing to see what sticks, but I am afraid (honestly) for what will happen when it all hits the fan after awhile.

But what will that look like?

> “If someone ever shows you their true colors, believe them.” - Linus Sebastian

I don’t know and I cannot predict the future. Who knows what the next breakthrough the AI Labs around the world will find, or maybe if this whole fun little experiement falls apart. What I do know, however, is that there is a very blatant showing of the true colors, the narrative stated outloud, that people have not fully touched on or realized yet concerning the devlopment of AI tooling today. The people who are truly in power, the executives, and leaders of the largest and most powerful corportations in the world are not afraid to replace the the common person with AI, expecially once the technology becomes more capable.

That is a huge concern for me and a disheartening fact around this technology. We have seen this happen in the technology industry at large and even at the tech-adjacent companies that exist as well. The risk isn’t just a theory anymore but an active change in the fabric of what the current economy looks like today. However, this is all done at a record pace without the checks and balances in place necessary to prevent systemic problems (at least seen in the technology sector). This sets a dangerous precedent for the future of knowledge work, especially when writing software systems that are critical to the daily lives of those at stake.

If and when these AI systems fuck up, what will happen? Who is the one to blame when it all goes wrong? I have asked this question to myself a number of times lately… keeping it in my meditations as of late. I suspect this is a lose-lose situation, with incentives that discourage presenting safety concerns around the use of this technology and instead promote the unregulated use all in the names of “quarterly reports” and arbitrary productivity metrics that do not mean anything. All in all, a dangerous place to be when the stakes can be high for system critical pieces of software.

In this case though, I am sincerely under the belief and impression that the blame for when this goes wrong will not be on the people who set these horrific incentives, but will fall on the shoulders of those who bare these unrealistic productivity expectations and are mandated to encorporate these AI toolsets without a stronger concern for double/triple checking the output of the models. Not to mention too, that there is also a growing trend to review pull requests with AI support, which doesnt help because the AI can lie and not catch all the individual places, it is not fully domain aware or contextually aware of alll the details… a lapse in knoweldge that may or may not be critical to working with the source code.

As I write this, I am lead to think that maybe I am just having a hard time adjusting to the new world, the new way things work in this knowledge work space. I feel that meme: “old man shakes fist at cloud,” as the world changes all around me. I do firmly believe, however, the concern that I share around the AI models in writing software is not just a minor concern. As we use more AI models, either by choice or forced into using these models due to work, the tasks become less and less thought provoking, and more-so button-pressing, dehumanizing, and ultimately a deteriation in the speciality that we as humans have worked at for so long to obtain.

The overuse of AI will soon be upon us, draining humnanity of the collective specialization and knowledge that we worked so hard to obtain. I fear we will one day lose that knowledge to our successors, the AI machines that will have access to all that knowledge we gathered over the course of many thousands of years as a species… and once that is to far gone, I doubt there will be a way to get it back.

I just hope one day we realize this before it is too late.
