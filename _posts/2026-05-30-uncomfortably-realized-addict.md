# The Internet is Coke.

I used to think that I was free from addiction. I don’t do drugs, I take my alcohol in moderation, I don’t do things that I shouldn’t. However, more recently, I’ve been without readily accessible internet… even cell service, disconnected. Sometimes, I’ll have a single bar… enough to get a notification from some app on my phone, a small hit. Yet, the overall connection is not strong enough to sustain anything substantial, such as a YouTube video or gaming online with freinds back home. On the surface level thought, you’d think this would be easy to deal with (or at least I had thought this in general), but actually being without internet completely… this is new.

Being in this position the last few days has gotten me to really think about my relationship with the internet. I have become addicted to always being connected and able to do things that involve the internet. There is so much of my day that depends on that connection, whether it is playing games like Marathon or haning out on DIscord… watching the latest podcast episode from my favorite creators, or listenting to music (at least the ones not readily downloaded, which became painfully aware). The moments when I wanted to look something up as simple as the weather. The lack of the readily available plethora of the world-wide-web… it hinders so much more than one first realizes at a glance.

As the hours, days went on… I noticed a rise in anxiety, an uneasiness: I am not connected, what if I am mising someting? What am I going to do with my free time? It was actually pathetic in a way, because there was realisticly plenty to do without internet… playing video games offline, reading, playing board games. These were things that don’t need an active connection, yet I had the feeling that there was nothing to do; which is an insane thing to think just because the internet was down. In general, I found this general feeling concerning. I am dependent on the internet at a level that left me uncomfortable. This is something that I want to change.

## We Lack the Acceptance of Slow Productivity

On a personally functional level, I feel that I should not always be connected to the internet, I do not need to always know what is going on or always have access to search… not everything needs an immediate answer. As a race, we have become accustomed to everything being present at our fingertips, with immediate results. That is a dangerous precedent… a source of anxiety when that expectation is not met. We need to be more tolerant of activities, updates, systems, data… all taking time. We need to slow down and take a breath as things process. I have thought this before, that I should be more ok with “slowness”, the idea of “slow productivity.” There was a good book a little ways back that I had finished on this very subject, and this lack of internet has really cemented the need or being accepting of the slow productivity movement, and apply it to my own life.

For nearly seven years, I have been a part of my profession, writing software. The industry now, albiet is kinda weird with the whole “AI” thing, but the core of the work remains the same as it has been since the begining of software engineering: deadlines. There are consistent processes, two-week cadences known as “sprints” where we plan out what we want to accomplish within those two weeks. Sometimes it’s a lot of stuff, sometimes it is more generally light-weight things, it ebbs and flows with resect to defects/bugs that can arise. However, that consistent expectation of continual progress and innovation is something that has an underlying reality; there is an unspoken rule about speed and efficiency. This unspoken expectation has seeped into my personal life, setting for some unrealistic aspirations for my own personal life, that being a consistent strain for progress.

This has been a struggle for me before, a driver for my anxiety… it has resulted in a dissastification with video games and hobbies that I have previously enjoyed. The drive saps the very human enjoyment of meaning in the activites, since it shifts focus away from the savoring of activity to the hyper fixation on the number of things accomplished. A dangerous precedant to being a human.

We are not machines, we do not do well when we aim to achieve the most things. Humans are emotional creatures, we take pride in the essence of things, the feeling of “meaning” in what we do. The really unhappy are those who do not find meaning in what they do, the people who treat their work as slavers, those who dont take moments to enjoy the essence. I find myself least happy when in that state.

So what does this mean?

## It is time to be… to be slow.

Now is the best time as any for anyone to just take the time to simply “be.” Taking a step back to just reflect, to soak in the essence of what we do. It is never about the sheer number of things accomplished, the enjoyment of creating something of great quality; we should capture the essence of the humanity in the activity. I do strongly believe that as we have further developed the society we live in, we have lost a bit of ourselves, lost the essence of just “being.” We are too caught up in what’s in the NOW, what is the LATEST, what is the NEXT, rather than what we are in this moment. We have become afraid to be bored, we need to bring back boredom and make it a regular part of our lives again.
