# Ditch it for RSS, Cowards.

## It’s not just you. There is a widespead problem.

Social Media was good once (I reflect fondly on 2013 Twitter days), when the world was in generally better shape. It was before the rampant enshitification of the internet, before the corporations that operate the internet had to focus on making a back their borrowed capital and before the time where the algorithm was tuned for peak addiction. Ads were not as prevelant and could actually find things on Google. Those were the days of peak internet.

Overtime though, the iron-cold grip of countless dollars reigned in the fun the corporations were allowed to have. Now, ads ads ads ads and misinformation (which gets views and taps) spans the WWW. With the power of “artificial intelligence”, you too can manipulate and potentially disrupt a small country’s stability and ability to trust in what they see. The content now just fucking sucks, with short-form for all the things (I mean even on YouTube of all places, it’s popular) dominating the mass media all thanks to the absolute unit of an algorithm hosted by TikTok. I have to give Bytedance the credit, that algo is very innovative and very impressive how fine tuned they got it.

This trend over the course of the last decade (wow, can you believe it’s been that long?) is the epitome of the frog-boiling parable, with the slow…but steady enshitification happening and everyone who’s not the megacorp underlying the tech being caught in the shitstorm. Before we knew it, we lost what we took for granted. Probably the biggest and largest nail in the coffin was the Twitter acquisition for me, but honestly insert your most relevant/favorite story here of how the internet fell apart for you, there’s plenty to go around. Since then, I have felt the facade shatter… that feeling of the “internet is fun” broke for me. It used to be the place of the cute flash games, coding forums, and a plethora of information (lets go wikipedia!). It was a place for me (as a teen) to see a tiny peephole into the vast world that was outside my immediate, sheltered bubble.

That is all changed now, and it sucks. It feels like nothing can be trusted. Take a look at the latest news on Google searches being filled with multi-page sponsored results (and a bunch of those being malware), or the countless number of people (whether it’s people you know personally or online horror stories) falling for scams or mis/disinformation driven by AI. Another example: the online harrassment by a swarm of bots or AI-generated “nudify” features promoted by Elon’s AI girlfriend Grok. Again, I am sure you know of a story or some new thing that could fit into this example.

All of this has contributed to a growing frustration and in some cases…hatred for what is going on right now in the internet. It’s all going to shit, so it seems…which SUCKS. Rather than staying connected to friends, I get to see weird religions imagery of “Shrimp Jesus” (look it up, I dare you). It has felt for a long time that the major platforms of social media of yesterday are now out to try, with full force, to make themselves as repulsive as possible and encourage repulsive behavior of their users…all for the sake of longer-than-average eyeballs being glued to the screen. Is this all really worth putting up for to be connected to people we don’t even know, in order to get some shiny magic internet points in the shape of an upward facing arrow for being an NPC (because God-forbid you say something the neaderthals of the mob dont like online)?

## So like… now what though?

I am a firm believer that it's not worth the time and effort and headspace to try to filter out the low-value versus high-value content that resides in the social media of today (not to mention there being other activities with greater fullfilment).Speaking from personal experience: how often did I really actually parttake in interacting with the content versus just being an enthusiastic lurker? Meditating on that question for AWHILE, honestly… most of the time, I would just scroll social media on my reverse-chonological feed to see what was the latest that was happening. No likes, no coments, just tapping links and reading the articles and seeing what some people I looked up to post their thoughts about. I also used the social media apps to see when the latest updates came out for games and the latest features came to the software I used everyday.

What I am describing I used the social media client for is exactly what an RSS Reader is designed to do, except social media has a large dash of negativity alongside all the links and articles… all that negativity was just weight on me, adding anxiety to my daily life. After awhile, it just wasn’t worth it anymore and I felt that there needed to be a change to take this weight off my mind so I could think more clearly and positivily.

The solution that I stumbled upon: RSS Feeds. This has been a technology that was kicking around for a long time, but something that I did not really use before. Once switching to that though, I have felt that my whole perspective of how to keep tabs on information, hobbies, topics, things that I love (but hate the discourse around online) has turned for a more positive outlook. Instead of lying to myself that I am in a pseudo-parasocial relationship with the people I “knew” online, I can instead focus on what is more valuable to me: the hobbies, games, topics, etc that I want to keep tabs on. Not to mention that there are a TON of RSS feed websites out there. Even Steam of all things has them for each game’s community updates.

In general, I know this isn’t necessarily for everyone, but if you are the kind of person who is feeling really down about all the things that are happening on social media, feel like the platforms have fallen apart, and want to break away from that constant negativity… I do encourage trying an RSS reader. There are many great ones out there, however, I do find that my favorite one is a macOS-only app (sorry to the non Apple users out there) called “Reeder.” It’s free to start, $10 per year for the “support the dev” version, and the interface is super easy to use. I have had it for awhile now.

As a user, you have the choice to not use the algorithm or participate in the constant negative that is modern social media. It’s also totally fine to stick with social media as well, either in addition to an RSS Reader or just social media at all. I just know that from my personal experience, an RSS Reader in place of social media has resulted in very good improvements in my anxiety, stress, and general happiness.
